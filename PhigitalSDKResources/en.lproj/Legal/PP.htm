4. Web Site Navigational Information
Phigital uses commonly-used information-gathering tools, such as cookies and Web beacons, to collect information as you navigate the Company's Web site ("Web Site Navigational Information"). This section describes the types of Web Site Navigational Information that may be collected on the Company's Web site and how this information may be used.
4.1 Cookies
Phigital uses cookies to make interactions with the Company's Web site easy and meaningful. When you visit the Company's Web site, Phigital's servers send a cookie to your computer. Standing alone, cookies do not personally identify you. They merely recognize your Web browser. Unless you choose to identify yourself to Phigital, either by responding to a promotional offer, opening an account, or filling out a Web form (such as a "Contact Me" or a "30 Day Free Trial" Web form), you remain anonymous to the Company. Phigital uses cookies that are session-based and persistent-based. Session cookies exist only during one session. They disappear from your computer when you close your browser software or turn off your computer. Persistent cookies remain on your computer after you close your browser or turn off your computer. If you have chosen to identify yourself to Phigital, the Company uses session cookies containing encrypted information to allow the Company to uniquely identify you. Each time you log into the Services, a session cookie containing an encrypted, unique identifier that is tied to your account is placed your browser. These session cookies allow the Company to uniquely identify you when you are logged into the Services and to process your online transactions and requests. Session cookies are required to use the Services. Phigital uses persistent cookies that only the Company can read and use to identify browsers that have previously visited the Company's Web site. When you purchase the Services or provide the Company with personal information, a unique identifier is assigned you. This unique identifier is associated with a persistent cookie that the Company places on your Web browser. The Company is especially careful about the security and confidentiality of the information stored in persistent cookies. For example, the Company does not store account numbers or passwords in persistent cookies. If you disable your Web browser's ability to accept cookies, you will be able to navigate the Company's Web site, but you will not be able to successfully use the Services. Phigital may use information from session and persistent cookies in combination with Data About Phigital Customers to provide you with information about the Company and the Services.
4.2 Web Beacons
Phigital uses Web beacons alone or in conjunction with cookies to compile information about Customers and Visitors' usage of the Company's Web site and interaction with emails from the Company. Web beacons are clear electronic images that can recognize certain types of information on your computer, such as cookies, when you viewed a particular Web site tied to the Web beacon, and a description of a Web site tied to the Web beacon. For example, Phigital may place Web beacons in marketing emails that notify the Company when you click on a link in the email that directs you to one of the Company's Web site. Phigital uses Web beacons to operate and improve the Company's Web site and email communications. Phigital may use information from Web beacons in combination with Data About Phigital Customers to provide you with information about the Company and the Services.
4.3 IP Addresses
When you visit Phigital's Web site, the Company collects your Internet Protocol ("IP") addresses to track and aggregate non-personal information. For example, Phigital uses IP addresses to monitor the regions from which Customers and Visitors navigate the Company's Web site.

5. Integration of 3rd Party features, technology and services
Phigital from time to time incorporates third-party applications into its Services in order to provide Customers and Visitors with important features and functionality. In using our Services you are agreeing to the named third parties collecting, using and storing information as described below.
5.1 Gimbal:
Phigital's mobile applications are built using functionality referenced as Gimbal which collects, uses and stores the following information:
5.1.1 Data Collection:
End-user Location fixes - latitude / longitude coordinates, accuracy estimate, time of day stamp and your location when/if your device is near any of Gimbal's public or private Bluetooth beacons â€“ small devices that may be placed, for example, in retail stores or other locations â€“ all of which are sourced from native OS location APIs. Location collection occurs periodically, based on significant location change events as reported by the native OS. Attributes of Wi-Fi networks visible by the device - BSSID and signal strength. Sourced from native OS Wi-Fi APIs.
Information about the end-user device (e.g., make, model, OS, and other similar information) but not phone number or UDID. Information about your version and use of the Gimbal software such as use of features, functions, or clicks on notifications or content. Information that end-users may enter in Phigital's application that is passed to Gimbal such as designating a private place (e.g., "my favorite store").
5.1.2 Data Use:
Gimbal monitors end-user location to create place events ("at" or "left"). These events are used to notify Phigital's app when the end-user has arrived at or left places designated in experiences that the user has chosen to join, "POIs". Gimbal generates a list of the location clusters (identified by lat/longs) that the end-user visits frequently and may use these lists to infer the places you visit most often, determine your frequency at these locations, and notify Phigital's App when you arrive at or leave those places. Gimbal may also use the collected information to provide, protect, and improve Gimbal and our systems and services which may include sharing your information with agents, service providers, vendors, contractors, or affiliates who process the data only on our behalf for the purposes set forth above; protect our rights and the rights of our users; de-identify, aggregate or otherwise make the data we collect anonymous and use that information for our business purposes. This privacy policy does not apply to data once it has been aggregated or de-identified.
5.1.3 Data Storage:
Location fixes that do not generate place events are not stored. The single location fix that does generate a place event is stored on the client, along with the corresponding place event. The set of generated POIs are stored on the client. Place events and the location fix that caused the event are stored on the Gimbal servers. The set of generated POIs are stored on Gimbal servers. Gimbal Servers are located in the United States. All data transmission is sent encrypted via SSL.
5.1.4 Data Retention:
Gimbal stores information on the client for up to sixty days, at which point it is overwritten on a rolling basis. Gimbal stores information on Gimbal managed servers for up to one year, at which point it is overwritten on a rolling basis.
